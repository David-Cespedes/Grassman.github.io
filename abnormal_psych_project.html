The disorder that I chose was Dissociative Idenity Disorder. (DID)

Definition of DID

The definition of DID is a disorder in which the person's mental process causes a lack of connection between their thoughts, memories, feelings, actions, or sense of identity.

To put it into shorter words; DID is basically a disorder where a person has multiple personalities, reason why it was earlier called Muliple Personality Disorder.

Symptoms of DID

The symptoms of DID are:

  • Memory loss of certain things (time periods, people, events, etc.)
  • A sense of being detached from yourself and your emotions
  • A blurred sense of identity
  • Stress in relationships, work, or other important things in your life
  • Not able to cope well with stress (emotional or professional)
  • Mental health problems (Depression, Anxiety, and Suicidal thoughts or behaviors.

Causes of DID

The causes of DID could be:

  • A traumatic past
  • Accidents
  • Natural Disasters
  • War
  • Loss of a parent
  • Long periods of isolation due to illness

Treatment

How to treat DID:

  • Psychotherapy (Talk Therapy)
  • Hypnotherapy (When hypnosis is used to access repressed memories, control some of the problematic behaviors, and to integrate all the personalities into one.)
  • Adjunctive Therapy (Art or movement therapy helps people connect with other parts of their mind that they have supressed to cope with trauma.)
